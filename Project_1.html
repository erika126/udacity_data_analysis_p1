1.The independent variable is the word condition (congruent vs. incongruent) and the dependent variable is the amount of time taken to perform the test

2.An appropriate null hypothesis is that the condition has no impact on the mean time required for the test, i.e. the mean time required by the population under the congruent condition is the same as the mean time required by the population under the incongruent condition.

In this case, the alternate hypothesis would be that the mean time taken under the two conditions is not the same.

We could do a dependent T-test on the difference in times between the two data conditions: congruent vs. incongruent. The T-test makes sense because we don't know any population statistics a priori, we only have our samples. Because the hypothesis does not state a direction, we will use a two-tailed test- we simply want to show that the condition has an impact on time required, regardless of whether it's slower or faster.
